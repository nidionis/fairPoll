Quick & Simple Voting

For everyday decisions or informal voting, use new quickpoll and share the poll ID with your participants.

Secure Voting

For more important decisions where you need to prevent ballot stuffing, mistakes, or server-side monitoring concerns, we recommend our secure House voting system.

How it works:

  1. Gather your voters: All voters must belong to the House.
  2. Create an house : and set it up with all its voting members. Note: To prevent ballot stuffing, you cannot add new voters after the House is created.
  3. Create the Poll: Initiate a new poll from within the House.
  4. Distribute Tickets: The poll creator must print, cut out, and randomly distribute the voting tickets (for example, by having members draw them from a hat).
  5. Cast Votes: Each voter uses their unique ticket to cast their vote anonymously.
  6. Review Results: Once voting ends, the results are stored in the House archives. The complete ballot can also be downloaded for independent verification.

Group Autonomy

FairPoll provides the tools, but your group remains in control. The group is entirely free to invalidate a vote according to its own agreed-upon rules (such as in the case of ties or ballot corruption).
