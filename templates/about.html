Quick & Simple Voting

  • For everyday decisions or informal voting, use new quickpoll and share the poll ID with your participants.
    • For several votes from the same device, use private navigation.
    A "quick poll" using tickets is actually quite secure if :
  • the tickets are kept secret until they are randomly assigned to users (drawing them from a box).
  • each ticket is strictly one per user (ensure it cannot be copied, such as by taking a picture or taking two of them).

Secure Voting

For more important decisions where you need to prevent ballot stuffing, mistakes, or server-side monitoring concerns, we recommend our secure House voting system.

How it works:

  • Gather your voters: All voters must belong to the House.
  • Create an house : and set it up with all its voting members.

    To prevent ballot stuffing, you cannot add new voters after the House is created.

  • Create the Poll: Initiate a new poll from within the House. An email is sent to each voter.
  • Distribute Tickets: The poll creator must print, cut out, and randomly distribute the voting tickets (for example, by having members draw them from a box).
  • Cast Votes: Each voter joins the poll using the poll ID, and uses their unique ticket to cast their vote anonymously (no signin requiered when voting a ticket poll).
  • Review Results: Once voting ends, the results are stored in the House archives. The complete ballot can be downloaded for independent verification.
