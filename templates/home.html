Security Concept

  • Anonymous Voting: You handle the distribution of unique physical tokens (e.g., via random draw). The server cannot link a ballot to a person.
  • Verifiable Results: The results are published and verifiable. Even the most skeptical voters can confirm how their vote was counted and review the calculations.

Group Autonomy

  • You Remain in Control: FairPoll provides the tools, but your group decides the outcome.
  • Custom Rules: The group is entirely free to invalidate a vote according to its own agreed-upon rules (such as in the case of ties or ballot corruption).
